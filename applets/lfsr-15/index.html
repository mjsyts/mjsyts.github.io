Rule: XOR the two right‑most bits (LSB & bit1), right‑shift, then insert the XOR result as the new left‑most bit (MSB).

How it works

  1. Take the two right‑most bits (bit0 = LSB and bit1).
  2. XOR them to get newBit.
  3. Right‑shift the 15‑bit integer by one.
  4. Insert newBit as the new left‑most bit (MSB).

This visualization highlights the two RMBs being XORed and animates the result moving to the MSB position before updating the register.

Built for demonstrating a simple 15‑bit LFSR (taps at bit0 and bit1). Not for cryptographic purposes.
